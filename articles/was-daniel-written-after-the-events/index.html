Was Daniel Written After The Events?

Late-date readings often arise from discomfort with predictive prophecy, but Daniel presents itself as exile-era prophecy grounded in God's rule over history.

Daniel 1:1-2Daniel 2:28Daniel 7:1Daniel 8:1Matthew 24:15

Why the question matters

Some readers argue that Daniel was written after many of the events it describes, especially after the Greek-era crisis. The issue is not merely academic. A late-date approach often changes prophecy into coded history and reduces the force of divine foreknowledge.

Daniel presents itself differently. It places the prophet in Babylon and Medo-Persia, dates visions in relation to kings, and repeatedly says that God reveals what will happen.

The book's own claim

Daniel 2 says God reveals secrets and makes known what shall be in the latter days. Daniel 7 and 8 are dated to Belshazzar's reign. Daniel 9 occurs after Babylon's fall. Daniel 10 begins in the third year of Cyrus. The narrative and visions are framed as revelation given across Daniel's life.

A reader may reject that claim, but the book itself should be allowed to speak before it is explained away.

Predictive prophecy is the theological issue

The late-date argument often assumes that detailed prediction cannot happen. But Daniel's theology rests on the opposite claim: the God of heaven gives kingdoms, removes kings, reveals secrets, and rules the future.

If predictive prophecy is possible, then detailed fulfillment is not evidence against Daniel. It is exactly what Daniel says the God of heaven can provide.

Jesus treats Daniel as prophecy

Jesus refers to Daniel in connection with the abomination of desolation. That matters because the New Testament does not treat Daniel as a failed or exhausted ancient text. Daniel still speaks forward in Jesus' teaching.

This does not answer every historical question by itself, but it places Christian reading under the authority of Christ's use of Daniel.

Why this matters

If Daniel is reduced to after-the-fact history, the book's central witness is weakened. The God who reveals the future becomes a literary device, and the long movement to judgment and final kingdom loses force.

The stronger reading takes Daniel's own claim seriously: heaven rules history, reveals what is coming, and calls God's people to faithfulness before the final kingdom arrives.
