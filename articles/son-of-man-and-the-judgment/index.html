The Son Of Man And The Judgment

Daniel 7 places Christ at the center of judgment: the court sits, the books open, and the Son of Man receives the kingdom before the saints receive it.

Daniel 7:9-14Daniel 7:21-27Matthew 26:64Acts 7:55-56Revelation 14:6-7

The court interrupts the beasts

Daniel 7 moves from beasts to judgment. The little horn speaks great words and makes war against the saints, but the vision does not end with the horn's noise. Thrones are set, the Ancient of Days sits, and the books are opened.

This shift is the heart of the chapter. Earthly powers look terrifying from below, but heaven is not passive. The court scene tells the reader that the beasts do not decide the final meaning of history.

The Son of Man receives dominion

Daniel sees One like the Son of Man coming with the clouds of heaven to the Ancient of Days. He receives dominion, glory, and a kingdom. His dominion is everlasting, and His kingdom will not be destroyed.

This is not merely another empire. The Son of Man is human enough to represent the saints and heavenly enough to come with the clouds. Jesus applies this language to Himself, placing His identity and authority inside Daniel's judgment scene.

The saints receive because Christ receives

Daniel 7 says the saints receive the kingdom, but only after the Son of Man receives dominion. That order is gospel-shaped. God's people do not conquer the beasts by becoming beastlike. They inherit the kingdom because their Representative receives it.

This guards the judgment from becoming self-centered anxiety. Judgment vindicates God's people because they belong to Christ. The court removes the dominion of the persecuting power and gives the kingdom to those united with the Son of Man.

Judgment is good news for the oppressed

For the little horn, judgment is exposure and removal. For the saints, judgment is vindication and deliverance. Daniel 7 does not present judgment as arbitrary terror, but as the moment when heaven answers oppression and false authority.

Revelation 14 announces the hour of God's judgment in the setting of the everlasting gospel. That is Daniel 7 language moving outward to the world. The Judge is also the Redeemer.

Why this matters

The Son of Man keeps Daniel's judgment scene centered on Christ. Prophecy is not merely about identifying beasts; it is about seeing who receives the kingdom after the beasts are judged.

The saints can endure because the final verdict does not belong to the horn. It belongs to the Ancient of Days, and the kingdom belongs to the Son of Man.
