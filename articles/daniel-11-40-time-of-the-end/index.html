Daniel 11:40 And The Time Of The End

Daniel 11:40 marks the time-of-the-end shift, where spiritual Egypt pushes against the northern power and the final conflict accelerates.

Daniel 11:40Daniel 12:1Revelation 11:7-8Revelation 13:3Revelation 17

The verse marks a new setting

Daniel 11:40 begins at the time of the end. That phrase should not be passed over. The prophecy has moved beyond the earlier literal north-south wars and into the closing movement that leads directly to Michael standing in Daniel 12:1.

This does not mean history disappears. It means the conflict has widened. The powers now represent larger spiritual and ideological systems.

The King of the South as spiritual Egypt

In the time-of-the-end setting, the King of the South is best understood as spiritual Egypt: open, atheistic, anti-biblical opposition to God and His word. Revelation 11 uses Egypt symbolically for a power that rejects God and opposes His witnesses.

The French Revolution gives a major historical example. Its rejection of biblical authority, assault on Christian witness, and secularizing force fit the southern push against the religious-political power.

The King of the North responds

The King of the North points to papal religious-political power, wounded around 1798 yet later recovering influence. Daniel describes a whirlwind-like response with chariots, horsemen, and many ships, imagery of sweeping counteraction and renewed dominance.

The verse is not merely predicting one battle. It sketches the reversal after the southern push: religious-political power regains strength and moves into a broader final campaign.

Revelation gives the wider picture

Revelation 13 speaks of a deadly wound that is healed and of the world wondering after the beast. Revelation 17 portrays kings joining with Babylon. These passages help explain why Daniel 11:40 matters: the final struggle involves recovered religious authority and global influence.

The southern power resists, but the northern power ends the verse with momentum. That sets up the sweeping movements of Daniel 11:41-45.

Why this matters

Daniel 11:40 is a bridge from the long historical conflict to the final crisis. It identifies the time of the end and shows the conflict moving from ancient geography into spiritual and ideological struggle.

The verse prepares the reader for Daniel 12:1. When the final powers reach their limit, Michael stands for His people.
