The Little Horn In Daniel 7 And 8

Daniel 7 and 8 identify the little horn by sequence, geography, character, actions, time period, and judgment outcome. Revelation develops the same pattern.

Daniel 7:8Daniel 7:24-26Daniel 8:9-14Revelation 13:5-7

Daniel 7 gives the main identifying marks

The little horn of Daniel 7 does not appear in isolation. It arises from the fourth beast, which follows Babylon, Medo-Persia, and Greece. In the broad sweep of Daniel 2 and 7, the fourth kingdom is Rome. The horn rises among the divided horns that come from that fourth beast, so its setting is post-Roman division.

Daniel adds further marks. The little horn appears after the ten horns, differs from the earlier political kingdoms, uproots three, speaks great words against the Most High, wears out the saints, thinks to change times and law, and dominates for time, times, and the dividing of time. Then the judgment sits and its dominion is taken away.

The evidence points to papal Rome

The biblical evidence points to papal Rome as the religious-political continuation of Rome. It rises in the Roman world after imperial fragmentation. It differs from ordinary kingdoms because it claims spiritual authority while exercising temporal influence. It speaks in religious terms, persecutes dissenting saints, and places human authority where God's word and law should rule.

The time period also matters. Daniel's time, times, and half a time matches Revelation's 1260 days and 42 months. In apocalyptic prophecy this points to a long period, not a literal three and a half years. The A.D. 538-1798 span fits the rise and wounding of papal supremacy in history.

Daniel 8 shows the sanctuary angle

Daniel 8 approaches the same Roman problem through sanctuary language. The little horn follows Medo-Persia and Greece, grows exceedingly great, moves toward the south, east, and pleasant land, and then magnifies itself against the Prince of the host. It casts truth to the ground and is involved in the sanctuary question.

This two-phase pattern fits Rome. Pagan Rome explains the horizontal imperial expansion. Papal Rome explains the vertical religious assault: counterfeit mediation, obscured truth, human priestly claims, tradition placed over Scripture, and a system that diverts attention from Christ's heavenly ministry.

Revelation develops the same power

Revelation 13 gathers Daniel's beast imagery and describes a power that speaks blasphemy, makes war with the saints, continues for 42 months, receives a deadly wound, and later receives renewed attention. The parallels with Daniel 7 are too strong to ignore.

Revelation does not cancel Daniel's sequence; it expands it. Daniel identifies the rise and career of the little horn. Revelation shows the final global worship crisis that grows out of the same conflict over authority, worship, truth, and allegiance.

Why this matters

The little horn is not a curiosity for prophetic charts. It exposes the danger of religious power joined to coercive authority. It warns against any system that replaces Christ's word, law, priesthood, and mediation with human control.

Daniel also gives hope. The little horn is judged. Its dominion is taken away. The kingdom belongs to the Son of Man and is given to the saints because Christ receives it first.
