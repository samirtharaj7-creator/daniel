Prophetic Schools

How interpreters read Daniel

Compare the major approaches to Daniel's prophecies and the evidence each approach emphasizes.

Sanctuary Historicism

A historicist reading that emphasizes the year-day principle, Daniel-Revelation connections, Christ's heavenly sanctuary ministry, 1844, and final worship conflict.

How it reads Daniel

Daniel is read as a Christ-centered apocalyptic book moving from exile to judgment, sanctuary restoration, and God's final kingdom.

Strengths

  • Integrates Daniel with sanctuary theology and Revelation
  • Keeps Christ and judgment central
  • Provides a coherent prophetic timeline

Weaknesses or limitations

  • Daniel 11 details remain debated
  • Requires careful page-level citations
  • Needs pastoral care when presenting judgment themes

SchoolDaniel ReadingMain Difference
HistoricismDaniel's visions provide a continuous outline of world empires, religious-political conflict, judgment, and the kingdom of God.This broad approach shares the continuous-history sequence, while readers differ on sanctuary, judgment, and date details.
Sanctuary HistoricismDaniel is read as a Christ-centered apocalyptic book moving from exile to judgment, sanctuary restoration, and God's final kingdom.This is the sanctuary-centered historicist framework used here.
PreterismDaniel's visions are frequently concentrated in the crisis of the second century BC and the experience of faithful Jews under oppression.Rejects or minimizes the continuous sequence through Rome, papal Rome, the 2300 years, and 1844.
FuturismDaniel is read as pointing strongly to an end-time antichrist, restored temple themes, and a final tribulation period.Moves key time periods into the future instead of reading them through continuous history.
Idealism / Symbolic InterpretationDaniel becomes a theological drama of empire, faithfulness, judgment, and hope repeated in many ages.Treats much of the material as recurring symbol rather than a specific prophetic sequence.
