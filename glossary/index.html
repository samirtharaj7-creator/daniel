Glossary

Symbols and key themes

Filter Daniel's symbols, prophetic terms, and study notes.

Ancient of Days

A title in Daniel 7 for God presiding in the heavenly judgment.

Study note: The scene points to the heavenly judgment before the final kingdom is given.

Daniel 7:9-10

Babylon

The first kingdom in Daniel's empire sequence and a symbol of human pride and false worship.

Study note: Babylon is both a historical empire and a later prophetic symbol.

Daniel 1:1Daniel 2:38Revelation 18:1-5

Little horn

A horn power in Daniel 7 and 8 that opposes God, truth, sanctuary, and saints.

Study note: The identifying marks point to the papal phase of Rome in Daniel 7, while Daniel 8 extends the conflict into sanctuary and truth language.

Daniel 7:8Daniel 7:25Daniel 8:9-12

Sanctuary

The biblical worship and mediation system that Daniel 8 places under attack and restoration.

Study note: Daniel 8:14 is read in relation to Christ's heavenly sanctuary ministry and the judgment-hour message.

Daniel 8:11-14Hebrews 8:1-2

Son of Man

The figure in Daniel 7 who comes with the clouds to the Ancient of Days and receives the kingdom.

Study note: This figure is Christ, who receives the kingdom in the heavenly judgment scene.

Daniel 7:13-14Matthew 26:64

Year-day principle

A prophetic interpretation principle that understands symbolic prophetic days as years.

Study note: This principle supports the 1260 days, seventy weeks, and 2300 days as prophetic time periods.

Numbers 14:34Ezekiel 4:6Daniel 8:14

2300 days

The prophetic period in Daniel 8:14, read as 2300 years ending in 1844.

Study note: This period is central to Daniel 8, the sanctuary, judgment, and the 1844 conclusion.

Daniel 8:14Daniel 9:24-27

Michael

The heavenly prince who stands for God's people in Daniel 12:1.

Study note: Michael is identified with Christ in His role as defender and deliverer of God's people.

Daniel 10:13Daniel 12:1Jude 9
